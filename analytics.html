Predicting Yelp Review Upvotes by Mining Underlying Topics

Rachel Shuyan Wang

When a restaurant has already have more than a hundred reviews on Yelp (or Youtube, Airbnb, etc), are you still willing to write a new one knowing that it might be buried deep? The lack of user engagement would reduce Yelp traffic, and slow down the metabolism of the website in the long run. This paper applied LDA and other topic modeling methods on the Yelp Academic Dataset to design a system that can automatically recommend potentially high quality reviews. It also discovers the positive correlation between the amount of 'useful' subtopics a review covers and its usefulness decided by the human annotators.

All code for this research project is written in R, including data cleaning, text parsing (text mining package 'tm'), statistical model ('lda' and 'topicmodels') and data visualization ('ggplot2', 'wordcloud', scatter plot, heatmap etc.).

An Analysis of Keywords in Social Media Posts and Their Implications

Rachel Shuyan Wang, Lleyana Jack & Jeeyoon Park

The purpose of text mining is to automate the navigation of text usually through statistical pattern matching algorithms and drastically reduce the time and cost of human labor. For this research, we used a dataset that contained Facebook, Twitter, and federal news release information for 2012. The visualized results showed that: 1) usage patterns for Facebook, Twitter and news are lowest on Mondays and Tuesdays. 2) And social media data, Facebook and Twitter, had higher positive, strong and weak word matches than news data. 3) There was a 5 hour lag time between news Release updates and Facebook for the same keywords. 4) The trending keywords show a close relationship with the timeline of major events.

I was responsible for parsing (Python) & analyzing Twitter data (R), clustering topics and use D3.js to develop an interactive Streamgraph (JavaScript) showing the frequency trend of keywords and the timeline of major events, code here.

Looking into Online Advertisement Opportunity History

Rachel Shuyan Wang

This is one of the reports I did during my summer internship as a data scientist in AOL. By not only analyzing opportunities won, but also the lost ones, the report has revealed the real reasons a seller loses a case and different stages to close an online ads deal, which are can be neglected by company stategists at times.

How Does Teachers’ Salary Influence Students’ Performance?

Rachel Shuyan Wang

This paper discussed the common phenomena in China that high schools’ qualities are experiencing major changes after the Great Reform and Opening. I collected and analyzed Economic data from two high schools in Luoyang Area using Stata. In the Difference-in-Differences model, year 2005 proved to be a turning point where the relative positions of two schools have changed significantly. There is also a chance that the rise in salary and larger salary gap are both affecting the academic performance of students. Teaching experience has proved to be a significant factor influencing the academic performance of students.
